Origin of Lego

Lego was founded in 1932 by Ole Kirk Christiansen in Billund, Denmark. The name "Lego" is derived from the Danish words "leg godt," meaning "play well." Originally producing wooden toys, Lego introduced its iconic plastic bricks in 1949. Since then, Lego has become a global phenomenon, inspiring creativity and learning in children and adults alike.

Structure of a Lego Brick

The classic Lego brick is a marvel of design. Each brick features precise dimensions and a unique interlocking system of studs and tubes, allowing for a snug fit and endless building possibilities. The standard 2x4 brick, for example, can be combined in over 915 million ways with just six bricks!
